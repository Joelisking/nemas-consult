DR. SAMUEL NKUMBAAN

Archaeological and Cultural Assessor


Dr. Nkumbaan holds a BA in Archaeology with History, an MPhil and a PhD in Archaeology all from the University of Ghana. He has Certificates and other training in Archaeological and Resource Management from the University of Copenhagen, Denmark as well as Academic Writings for Graduate Students from the Michigan University, USA.
Nkumbaan, who speaks about 4 different Ghanaian dialects, is an astute archaeologist with extensive experience in Human Origins and Cultural Foundations in Africa, Archaeology and Tourism in Ghana, Cultural Resources Management in Ghana, Archaeology and the Natural Environment, Anthropology, Peace and Conflict Studies, Cultural Evolution, and Human Diversity. He has additional training in Field Conservation for Iron and Bronze Age artefacts and Archaeological and Cultural Heritage Resources Management from the University of Ghana, University of Copenhagen-Denmark, and Manchester University in the United Kingdom. These study tours took him to Museums, Heritage Institutions, and Archaeological Sites in the United Kingdom and Denmark. He was part of a team of Archaeologists that surveyed and recorded Islamic Architectural Monuments in Northern Ghana, and Iron Age Archaeological Sites in Togo and Benin for the University of Copenhagen, Denmark under the auspices of the Ghana-Denmark Archaeological Research (GDarch) Project. His other archaeological research experiences include visits to the Hopewell Culture sites in Ohio, Heritage Institutions and Museums in Michigan, and particularly the Henry Ford Heritage Facilities in Detroit, all in the USA. He has been part of the Central Region (Ghana) Archaeological Research Project directed by Syracuse University, USA and The Kintampo Archaeological Research Project in Ghana, conducted by University College London, UK. In addition, he has conducted a number of contract Archaeological Research Projects for agencies including Newmont Ghana Gold Limited, African Environmental Research Consult (AERC), Ghana Highways Authority and UNESCO. Several international institutions have recognized the works of Dr. Nkumbaan. They are the African Studies Centre of the University of Michigan which awarded him the African Presidential Scholars (UMAPS) fellowship for his PhD dissertation; the GDArch Project, DANIDA that funded his study tours in Denmark and conferences in Senegal and India; and the British Academy, which funded his expenses to mount an exhibition of the Komaland Archaeological Research findings at the University of Manchester. In addition to his academic, research and consultancy drive, Dr. Nkumbaan has been actively involved with community development and advocacy for good governance practices in Ghana and is currently the Convenor for the Forum of Governance and Justice (FGJ), a Civil Society Organization; and the Director of Concern Voice Ghana, an NGO.

About Us

NEMAS CONSULT LIMITED is an Environmental, Social and Safety Impacts and Risks Management consulting firm with its head office in Accra, Ghana. We offer end-to-end EHSS solutions that best support our clients' business needs and suit their particular situations, including regulatory requirements, environmental and social due diligence, environmental permitting, environmental compliance, environmental management, occupational health and safety, natural resources management, and environmental laboratory analysis services.
