EBENEZER ADOTEY-ALLOTEY

Hydrologist


Ebenezer Adotey-Allotey holds a B.Sc. in Physics from the University of Science and Technology, Kumasi (1981), a Postgraduate Diploma in Hydrological Engineering from the Institute of Hydraulic & Environmental Engineering, Delft, Netherlands (1987), and an Advanced Certificate in Water Resources Management from the Università per Stranieri, Perugia, Italy (1991). He has also received specialized training in Solar Salt Farm Design and Construction under UNDP in 1988, participated in the first Environmental Impact Assessment Workshop hosted by UST Kumasi and Central State University, Ohio, in 1994, and completed a refresher course on Integrated Water Resources and Wetlands Management in Cape Town, South Africa, in 1996. In 1999, he studied new techniques in surface water hydrology in Montpellier, France, followed by advanced leadership training in Singapore in 2001, and regional watershed management training in Tanzania in 2002.

Ebenezer Adotey-Allotey is a seasoned hydrologist and water resources engineer with over 30 years of expertise across urban and applied hydrology, drainage system design, environmental studies, and water management. His extensive career includes consulting for prominent organizations in the Mining, Aviation and other reputed organizations. He has led numerous hydrologic and hydraulic studies since 1996, focusing on flood management, hydropower feasibility assessments on the Ankobra and Pra Rivers, and irrigation projects in Ghana’s Upper East and Northern Regions. His diverse portfolio also includes solar salt farm development, complex hydrologic and hydraulic analyses for industrial, urban, and agricultural sectors, airport drainage redesigns, and environmental impact assessments.

His notable contributions include water management planning for reservoirs, the redesign of drainage systems for aviation industries. His portfolio includes major projects such as leading hydrological studies for environmental impact assessments, flood relief coordination, and drainage infrastructure construction. He has played key roles in projects like the Korle Lagoon dredging, small-town drainage master planning, and rail line hydrological assessments. A member of the International Association of Hydrological Sciences (IAHS), Mr. Allotey’s career demonstrates a deep proficiency in hydrological science and environmental engineering, with a focus on sustainable water resource management in Ghana.
